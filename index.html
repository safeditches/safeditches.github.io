There Are Alternatives

Other viable methods have been used to control weeds and algae, but are not currently being used by NID.

NID has composed a working group to research alternative methods of terrestrial bank and berm vegitation control, however this working group does not address the use of aquatic herbicides, and is not testing the use of excavators for mechanical cleaning.

To find out more, please contact us at SafeDitches@gmail.com

Get Involved

We're all in this together, working to create cleaner, safer ditches for people, wildlife, livestock, pets, crops and gardens.

Here is how to get involved:

Vote

Vote in the November election. We support Bruce Herring and Laura Peters for the NID Board of Directors.

See more information about the upcoming elections here: Election Page

Request No Spray

Request that the ditch on your property is not sprayed. Keep roundup off your property by making your home a no spray zone.

Participate

Come to local NID Board of Director's meetings, times are listed here, or contact your district’s representative, contact information is listed here. Be sure to mention your stance on the use of aquatic and terrestrial herbicides in Nevada County’s ditches, and your opinion on the Centennial Dam project.

Take Photos

Being careful not to trespass, please send us pitcutres (SafeDitches@gmail.com) of NID workers spraying Roundup on the ditch banks and berms and of the 65 aluminum drip boxes. Be sure to tell us were the pictures came from! Pictures will be used to enhance this website and contribute to Mike Pasner’s map project.
