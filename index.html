A substance that is capable of causing the illness or death of a living organism when introduced or absorbed.

Get Involved

We're all in this together, working to create cleaner, safer ditches for people, wildlife, livestock, pets, crops and gardens.

Here is how to get involved:

Vote

Vote in the November election. We support Bruce Herring and Laura Peters for the NID Board of Directors.

Request No Spray

Request that the ditch on your property is not sprayed. Keep roundup off your property by making your home a no spray zone.

Participate

Come to local NID meetings, times are listed here, or contact your district’s representative, contact information is listed here. Be sure to mention your stance on the use of aquatic and terrestrial herbicide in Nevada County’s ditches, and your opinion on the Centennial Dam project.

Take Photos

Send us pitcutres (SafeDitches@gmail.com) of NID workers spraying Roundup on the ditch banks and berms, and of the 65 aluminum drip boxes. Be sure to tell us were the pictures came from! Pictures will be used to enhance this website and contribute to Mike Pasner’s map project.
